perspective

How I think about AI

I think the interesting challenge in AI right now is not raw intelligence — it's coordination, continuity, and controlled behavior over time. Single-turn AI is largely solved. What's hard is making AI systems that reliably do real work across sessions, tools, and teams.

That's why I gravitate toward agentic systems — multi-agent architectures, memory design, orchestration, and the operational patterns that make AI useful rather than impressive. I care less about what a model can do in a demo and more about what it can do on Tuesday morning when context is stale and the task is ambiguous.

As a leader, I bring this same lens to teams: how do we make AI a real capability in our engineering practice, not just a feature we bolt on? How do we build systems and processes that actually improve because AI is part of them?
